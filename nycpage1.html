The Empire State Building
The 1,250-foot, 102-story Art Deco wonder was the tallest building in the world at the time of its completion, on May 1, 1931.
Times Square
Nicknamed - The Crossroads of the World , a third of a million people pass through Times Square everyday. On February, 2017 it was declared smoke-free.
Central Park
The most visited urban park in the United States, Central Park is bigger than some countries. The park hosts 26,000 trees and seven water-bodies.
Statue of Liberty
Lady Liberty wears a size 879 shoe and has 25 windows in her crown. The 7 spikes in her crown represent the 7 continents and oceans in the world.
